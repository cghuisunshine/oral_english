# IELTS Speaking Question Bank With Model Answers

Source index: https://ielts.koolearn.com/20250812/866639.html  
Scope: 2025 September-December IELTS Speaking topics listed by New Oriental Online.  
Note: These are original practice answers, not official examiner answers and not copied from the source.

## How To Use This Bank

Practice Part 1 in 20-30 seconds per answer. Practice Part 2 by preparing for 1 minute and speaking for 1-2 minutes. Use the Part 3 follow-up to extend your answer with reasons, comparisons, and examples.

## Part 1 Quick Interview Bank

1. **Study or work**  
   Question: Do you work or are you a student?  
   Answer: I am a student at the moment, and I am focusing mainly on English and academic skills. My schedule is quite full, but I like it because I can see steady progress.

2. **Hometown**  
   Question: What is your hometown like?  
   Answer: It is a busy but friendly city with a mix of modern buildings and older neighborhoods. I like it because daily life is convenient and there are many places to eat and relax.

3. **Accommodation**  
   Question: Do you live in a house or an apartment?  
   Answer: I live in an apartment. It is not very large, but it is comfortable, easy to clean, and close to shops and public transport.

4. **The area you live in**  
   Question: What do you like about your neighborhood?  
   Answer: I like the convenience most. There are cafes, grocery stores, and parks nearby, so I can handle most daily tasks without spending much time travelling.

5. **Public places**  
   Question: What public places do people often visit in your city?  
   Answer: People often go to parks, shopping centers, libraries, and sports facilities. Parks are especially popular because they are free and suitable for all ages.

6. **Doing something well**  
   Question: Can you describe something you did well recently?  
   Answer: I recently gave a short presentation in English, and it went better than I expected. I prepared clear notes, spoke slowly, and answered questions confidently.

7. **Shoes**  
   Question: What kind of shoes do you usually wear?  
   Answer: I usually wear comfortable sneakers because I walk quite a lot every day. I care more about comfort than fashion, although I still prefer a simple, clean style.

8. **Rules**  
   Question: Are rules important in daily life?  
   Answer: Yes, rules make shared spaces safer and more organized. For example, traffic rules help people move around without confusion or unnecessary risk.

9. **Working with old people**  
   Question: Have you ever worked with older people?  
   Answer: Yes, I have helped older relatives with phones and online services. It taught me to explain things patiently and avoid using too much technical language.

10. **Crowded place**  
    Question: Do you like crowded places?  
    Answer: Not really. I can accept crowds at concerts or festivals, but in daily life I prefer quieter places because they help me think and relax.

11. **Growing vegetables**  
    Question: Would you like to grow vegetables?  
    Answer: Yes, I would like to try growing herbs or tomatoes on a balcony. It seems relaxing, and it would be satisfying to eat something I grew myself.

12. **Advertising**  
    Question: Are you influenced by advertisements?  
    Answer: Sometimes, especially when an ad shows a product clearly and gives useful information. However, I usually check reviews before buying anything expensive.

13. **Chatting**  
    Question: Do you prefer chatting online or face to face?  
    Answer: For quick messages, online chatting is more convenient. But for serious conversations, I prefer face to face because tone and body language make things clearer.

14. **Taking a break**  
    Question: How do you usually take a break?  
    Answer: I usually take a short walk, drink water, or listen to music. Even ten minutes away from a screen can help me feel more focused.

15. **Sharing**  
    Question: Do you like sharing things with others?  
    Answer: Yes, especially food, notes, and useful information. Sharing makes relationships warmer, and it often encourages others to help you as well.

16. **Plants**  
    Question: Do you keep plants at home?  
    Answer: I keep a small indoor plant near my desk. It is easy to care for, and it makes the room feel fresher and less plain.

17. **Friends**  
    Question: How often do you meet your friends?  
    Answer: I meet close friends once or twice a month because everyone is busy. We usually eat together or have a long conversation in a cafe.

18. **Museums**  
    Question: Do you enjoy visiting museums?  
    Answer: Yes, if the exhibition is well designed. I like museums that use stories, objects, and interactive displays instead of only long written descriptions.

19. **Going out**  
    Question: Do you often go out in the evening?  
    Answer: Not very often during the week, because I prefer to rest after studying or working. On weekends, I enjoy going out for dinner or a movie.

20. **Flowers**  
    Question: Do people in your country give flowers as gifts?  
    Answer: Yes, flowers are common for birthdays, graduations, and visits to someone who is ill. They are simple but thoughtful gifts.

21. **Puzzles**  
    Question: Do you like doing puzzles?  
    Answer: Yes, I like word puzzles and simple logic games. They are a good way to train concentration without feeling like serious study.

22. **Public transport**  
    Question: Do you often use public transport?  
    Answer: Yes, I use buses or trains when the route is convenient. It is cheaper than driving and I can read or listen to podcasts on the way.

23. **Names**  
    Question: Does your name have a special meaning?  
    Answer: Yes, my name was chosen by my family and it carries a positive meaning. I like it because it feels personal and connects me with my background.

24. **Geography**  
    Question: Did you enjoy geography at school?  
    Answer: I enjoyed parts of it, especially learning about countries, climates, and maps. It made travel and world news easier to understand.

25. **Birthday**  
    Question: How do you usually celebrate your birthday?  
    Answer: I usually keep it simple. I have dinner with family or friends, take a few photos, and maybe buy myself something small.

26. **Staying up late**  
    Question: Do you often stay up late?  
    Answer: I try not to, but sometimes I stay up late when I have deadlines. The next day I usually feel less energetic, so I am trying to sleep earlier.

27. **Jewellery**  
    Question: Do you often wear jewellery?  
    Answer: Not often. I prefer simple accessories, such as a watch or a plain ring, because they are practical and do not attract too much attention.

28. **Plans**  
    Question: Do you like making plans?  
    Answer: Yes, especially for study and travel. A plan helps me use time better, although I still leave some space for unexpected changes.

29. **Texting messages**  
    Question: Do you text people often?  
    Answer: Yes, I text every day for quick communication. It is useful for arranging meetings, sharing links, or checking small details.

30. **Snacks**  
    Question: What snacks do you like?  
    Answer: I like fruit, nuts, and sometimes chocolate. I prefer snacks that are easy to carry and do not make me feel too full.

31. **Patience**  
    Question: Are you a patient person?  
    Answer: I am fairly patient with people, but less patient with slow technology. I am trying to improve because impatience rarely solves the problem faster.

32. **Machine**  
    Question: What machine do you often use?  
    Answer: I use my laptop every day for studying, writing, and online meetings. It is probably the most important machine in my daily life.

33. **Memory**  
    Question: Do you have a good memory?  
    Answer: I have a good memory for stories and conversations, but I sometimes forget small tasks. That is why I use lists and calendar reminders.

34. **Weather**  
    Question: What kind of weather do you like?  
    Answer: I like mild, sunny weather because it is comfortable for walking and outdoor activities. I do not enjoy extreme heat or heavy rain.

35. **Writing**  
    Question: Do you write by hand often?  
    Answer: Not as often as before, because most work is digital now. However, I still write notes by hand when I want to remember something clearly.

36. **Being alone**  
    Question: Do you like spending time alone?  
    Answer: Yes, I need some time alone to recharge. I usually read, organize my room, or think through my plans for the week.

37. **Social media**  
    Question: Do you use social media often?  
    Answer: I use it almost every day, mainly to follow news, friends, and learning content. I try not to scroll for too long because it can waste time.

38. **Making a list**  
    Question: Do you often make lists?  
    Answer: Yes, I make to-do lists when I have several tasks. Lists reduce stress because I do not have to keep everything in my head.

39. **Housekeeping and cooking**  
    Question: Do you like cooking?  
    Answer: I like simple cooking because it is healthier and cheaper than eating out. I am not an expert, but I can make a few basic dishes.

40. **Saying thank you**  
    Question: When do you say thank you?  
    Answer: I say thank you whenever someone helps me, gives advice, or does something thoughtful. Small expressions of gratitude make daily interactions nicer.

41. **Chocolate**  
    Question: Do you like chocolate?  
    Answer: Yes, but I eat it in small amounts. Dark chocolate is my favorite because it is less sweet and has a richer taste.

42. **Singing**  
    Question: Do you like singing?  
    Answer: I enjoy singing casually, especially when I am alone or with close friends. I am not a great singer, but it helps me relax.

43. **Outer space and stars**  
    Question: Are you interested in outer space?  
    Answer: Yes, I find it fascinating because it shows how huge the universe is. I like documentaries about planets, stars, and space exploration.

44. **Small business**  
    Question: Would you like to run a small business?  
    Answer: Maybe in the future. A small business would give me freedom, but it would also require planning, money, and strong responsibility.

45. **Being busy**  
    Question: Do you like being busy?  
    Answer: I like being productively busy, because it gives my day structure. But if I am too busy for too long, I feel tired and less creative.

46. **Internet**  
    Question: How often do you use the internet?  
    Answer: I use it every day for study, communication, and entertainment. It is extremely useful, but I need discipline to avoid distractions.

47. **Praising**  
    Question: Do you often praise other people?  
    Answer: I try to praise people when they genuinely do something well. A sincere compliment can build confidence and make cooperation easier.

## Part 2 And Part 3 Cue-Card Bank

### People

1. **A friend who is good at music or singing**  
   Cue card: Describe a friend who is good at music or singing.  
   Model answer: I would describe my friend Lily, who sings beautifully and also plays the guitar. I first heard her at a small school event, and her voice immediately impressed me because it was warm and controlled. What I admire most is that she practices consistently instead of relying only on talent. Listening to her makes me feel calm and proud of her.  
   Part 3: Why do people enjoy music?  
   Answer: Music helps people express emotions that are hard to explain in ordinary words. It can also create shared memories at weddings, concerts, or family gatherings.

2. **An important good friend**  
   Cue card: Describe a good friend who is important to you.  
   Model answer: My important friend is someone I met at school. We became close because we studied together and supported each other during stressful exams. He is important to me because he is honest, reliable, and willing to tell me the truth even when it is uncomfortable. Our friendship makes me feel more confident.  
   Part 3: What makes a friendship last?  
   Answer: Trust and regular communication are essential. People can be busy, but if they still care about each other's problems and achievements, the friendship can stay strong.

3. **A person who runs a family business**  
   Cue card: Describe someone who runs a family business.  
   Model answer: I know a neighbor who runs a small bakery with her parents. She handles online orders while her parents focus on baking and customer service. I admire her because she mixes traditional recipes with modern marketing. The business feels personal, and customers trust it because the family clearly cares about quality.  
   Part 3: What are the advantages of family businesses?  
   Answer: Family businesses often have strong trust among members and a clear long-term purpose. However, they still need professional rules to avoid personal conflicts.

4. **A friend with good habits**  
   Cue card: Describe a friend who has good habits.  
   Model answer: One of my friends has a very healthy routine. He wakes up early, exercises, reads a little every day, and keeps his phone away during study time. I admire him because his habits are simple but consistent. He has shown me that discipline is not dramatic; it is built through small daily choices.  
   Part 3: How can people build good habits?  
   Answer: People should start with small actions and repeat them at the same time each day. If the habit is too difficult at first, they will probably give up.

5. **A creative person you admire**  
   Cue card: Describe a creative person you admire.  
   Model answer: I admire a designer I follow online because she can turn ordinary problems into elegant solutions. Her work is not only beautiful but also practical. What impresses me most is her ability to observe daily life carefully and find new ideas in simple things. She reminds me that creativity can be trained.  
   Part 3: Is creativity important at work?  
   Answer: Yes, because many jobs require people to solve new problems. Creativity is useful not only in art but also in business, teaching, and technology.

6. **A popular person**  
   Cue card: Describe a popular person you know.  
   Model answer: I know a classmate who is popular because she is easy to talk to and treats people fairly. She is not loud or attention-seeking, but she remembers people's names and listens carefully. I think people like her because she makes others feel comfortable rather than trying to impress them.  
   Part 3: Why are some people popular?  
   Answer: Some people are popular because they are friendly, dependable, and emotionally intelligent. Popularity based only on appearance or status usually does not last.

7. **A person who likes growing plants**  
   Cue card: Describe a person who enjoys growing plants.  
   Model answer: My aunt loves growing plants on her balcony. She grows flowers, herbs, and a few vegetables in small pots. I like watching her care for them because she is patient and gentle. Her balcony has become a peaceful corner of the home, and it shows how even a small space can feel alive.  
   Part 3: Why do people enjoy gardening?  
   Answer: Gardening gives people a sense of control and patience. It also offers a break from screens and makes the home environment more pleasant.

8. **A smart person you know**  
   Cue card: Describe an intelligent person you know.  
   Model answer: I would describe one of my teachers. He is smart not only because he knows a lot, but because he explains complex ideas clearly. He uses examples from daily life, so students can actually understand. I respect him because his intelligence helps other people learn, not just show off knowledge.  
   Part 3: What is the difference between knowledge and intelligence?  
   Answer: Knowledge is information someone has learned, while intelligence is the ability to use information effectively. A person needs both to solve real problems.

9. **A person who encouraged you to do something interesting**  
   Cue card: Describe someone who encouraged you to do an interesting activity.  
   Model answer: A friend encouraged me to join a public speaking club. At first I was nervous, but she said it would help my confidence. I tried it and slowly became more comfortable speaking in front of others. I am grateful because she pushed me gently without making me feel embarrassed.  
   Part 3: Should parents encourage children to try new activities?  
   Answer: Yes, but encouragement should not become pressure. Children need opportunities to explore, but they also need the freedom to stop if something is truly unsuitable.

10. **A favorite childhood friend**  
    Cue card: Describe a friend you liked when you were a child.  
    Model answer: My favorite childhood friend lived in the same building as me. We played games after school, shared snacks, and created small adventures in the neighborhood. I remember him because our friendship was simple and natural. We did not need expensive toys; we just enjoyed spending time together.  
    Part 3: Are childhood friendships different from adult friendships?  
    Answer: Yes. Childhood friendships are often based on play and closeness, while adult friendships need more effort because people have responsibilities and limited time.

11. **A person who wears unusual clothes**  
    Cue card: Describe someone who wears unusual clothes.  
    Model answer: I once met a student who often wore vintage jackets and bright colors. His style was unusual but confident, and it suited his artistic personality. I respected him because he did not dress strangely just for attention; he used clothing to express who he was.  
    Part 3: Should people be judged by their clothes?  
    Answer: Clothes can give a first impression, but they do not show someone's full character. It is unfair to judge ability or kindness only from appearance.

12. **A person from a different culture**  
    Cue card: Describe someone you know from a different culture.  
    Model answer: I met an exchange student from another country during a language activity. We talked about food, school, festivals, and family habits. The conversation was memorable because it made me notice assumptions I had never questioned. Knowing her helped me become more open-minded.  
    Part 3: Why is cultural exchange useful?  
    Answer: It reduces stereotypes and helps people communicate better. When people understand different customs, they are less likely to misunderstand each other.

13. **A friend you disliked at first but later liked**  
    Cue card: Describe a friend you did not like at first but later liked.  
    Model answer: At first, I thought a classmate was cold because he rarely smiled. Later, we worked on a project together and I discovered he was just shy and very responsible. After that, we became good friends. This experience taught me not to judge people too quickly.  
    Part 3: Why do first impressions sometimes change?  
    Answer: First impressions are based on limited information. Once people spend more time together, they can understand personality, background, and intentions better.

14. **A well-dressed friend**  
    Cue card: Describe a friend who dresses well.  
    Model answer: My friend has a simple but polished dressing style. She chooses clothes that fit well and match the occasion, rather than wearing expensive brands. I think she dresses well because she understands herself and pays attention to details. Her style looks confident without being excessive.  
    Part 3: Why do people care about appearance?  
    Answer: Appearance affects first impressions and can show respect for an occasion. However, it should not become more important than ability or character.

15. **Someone you would like to work or study with**  
    Cue card: Describe someone you want to work or study with.  
    Model answer: I would like to study with a classmate who is organized and calm under pressure. She makes clear plans, divides tasks fairly, and explains ideas patiently. I think working with her would be productive because she focuses on solutions instead of complaining.  
    Part 3: What qualities make a good teammate?  
    Answer: A good teammate is reliable, communicative, and flexible. Skills matter, but attitude often decides whether a team works smoothly.

16. **A person who persuaded you to do something**  
    Cue card: Describe someone who persuaded you to do something.  
    Model answer: My brother persuaded me to start exercising regularly. He did not lecture me; he simply invited me to walk with him every evening. After a few weeks, I felt healthier and slept better. I appreciated his approach because it was practical and encouraging.  
    Part 3: Is persuasion always positive?  
    Answer: No. Persuasion is positive when it respects people's choices and helps them make better decisions, but it becomes harmful when it manipulates or pressures them.

### Objects And Ideas

17. **A wild animal you want to know more about**  
    Cue card: Describe a wild animal you want to know more about.  
    Model answer: I would like to know more about dolphins. I saw them in a documentary and was impressed by their intelligence and social behavior. They seem playful, but they also communicate in complex ways. I want to learn how they cooperate and how humans can protect their ocean environment.  
    Part 3: Why should people protect wild animals?  
    Answer: Wild animals are part of the natural balance. Protecting them also protects ecosystems that humans depend on for clean water, food, and climate stability.

18. **An old object at home**  
    Cue card: Describe an old object in your home.  
    Model answer: There is an old photo album in my home that my family has kept for many years. The photos are slightly faded, but they show relatives, festivals, and places from the past. It is valuable because it preserves family history and starts many conversations.  
    Part 3: Why do people keep old things?  
    Answer: Old things often carry memories and emotional value. They remind people of family, personal growth, or important periods in life.

19. **A useful book**  
    Cue card: Describe a useful book you have read.  
    Model answer: A useful book I read was about building better habits. It explained how small routines can shape long-term results. I liked it because the advice was practical, not abstract. After reading it, I changed how I plan my study time and became more consistent.  
    Part 3: Are printed books still important?  
    Answer: Yes. Digital books are convenient, but printed books are easier on the eyes for many people and can help readers concentrate without notifications.

20. **A childhood toy**  
    Cue card: Describe a toy you liked in childhood.  
    Model answer: I loved a set of building blocks when I was a child. I used them to create houses, bridges, and imaginary cities. The toy was simple, but it encouraged creativity and problem-solving. I remember it because I could play with it for hours without getting bored.  
    Part 3: What toys are good for children?  
    Answer: Good toys should encourage imagination, movement, or thinking. They do not need to be expensive if they help children explore and learn.

21. **A beautiful object**  
    Cue card: Describe an object you think is beautiful.  
    Model answer: I think a handmade ceramic cup I bought from a market is beautiful. It has an uneven shape and a soft blue glaze, which makes it feel personal. I like it because it is both useful and artistic. Every small imperfection shows that someone made it carefully by hand.  
    Part 3: Is beauty only about appearance?  
    Answer: No. Beauty can also come from meaning, function, and craftsmanship. An object may become beautiful because of the story behind it.

22. **A photo you are proud of**  
    Cue card: Describe a photo you are proud of.  
    Model answer: I am proud of a photo I took during a sunset walk. The light, clouds, and reflections came together perfectly. I did not use professional equipment, but I waited for the right moment. The photo reminds me that patience can make ordinary scenes special.  
    Part 3: Why do people like taking photos?  
    Answer: Photos help people record experiences and share them with others. They also allow people to notice details they might otherwise ignore.

23. **A talent you want to improve**  
    Cue card: Describe a talent you want to improve.  
    Model answer: I want to improve my public speaking. I can explain ideas in writing, but speaking naturally under pressure is harder. I am practicing by recording myself and joining discussions. This talent matters because clear speaking can help in interviews, presentations, and everyday communication.  
    Part 3: Can talent be learned?  
    Answer: Natural ability helps, but most talents improve through practice and feedback. Consistency is usually more important than being gifted at the beginning.

24. **A traditional story**  
    Cue card: Describe a traditional story you know.  
    Model answer: One traditional story I remember is about honesty and keeping promises. I heard it from my family when I was young. Although the story is simple, it teaches that trust is difficult to rebuild once it is lost. I think that message is still useful today.  
    Part 3: Why are traditional stories important?  
    Answer: They pass values and cultural memory from one generation to another. They also teach lessons in a way that is easy for children to remember.

25. **A science subject you are interested in**  
    Cue card: Describe a science subject you are interested in.  
    Model answer: I am interested in astronomy because it makes me think beyond daily life. Learning about planets, stars, and space exploration gives me a sense of curiosity. I also like how astronomy combines observation, mathematics, and imagination. It makes the universe feel mysterious but understandable.  
    Part 3: Should children learn science early?  
    Answer: Yes, because science encourages curiosity and logical thinking. Early exposure can help children ask better questions about the world.

26. **A water sport**  
    Cue card: Describe a water sport you would like to try.  
    Model answer: I would like to try kayaking. It looks exciting but not too extreme, and it would allow me to enjoy nature from the water. I would prefer to learn it on a calm lake first. The sport appeals to me because it combines exercise, balance, and scenery.  
    Part 3: Why do some people enjoy risky sports?  
    Answer: Risky sports give people excitement and a sense of achievement. However, proper training and safety rules are necessary to keep the risk under control.

27. **A film you recently watched and want to watch again**  
    Cue card: Describe a recent film you would like to watch again.  
    Model answer: I recently watched a film with a strong story and excellent acting. I want to watch it again because I think I missed some details the first time. The characters felt realistic, and the ending stayed in my mind. A second viewing would help me understand it more deeply.  
    Part 3: Why do people rewatch films?  
    Answer: People rewatch films for comfort, entertainment, or deeper understanding. A good film can reveal new details when people watch it again.

28. **A skill you can teach others**  
    Cue card: Describe a skill you can teach other people.  
    Model answer: I can teach people how to organize study notes. I usually divide notes into key ideas, examples, and review questions. This method helps information become easier to remember. I could teach it because it is practical and does not require special tools.  
    Part 3: What skills should schools teach?  
    Answer: Schools should teach communication, critical thinking, digital skills, and basic financial knowledge. These skills help students handle real-life situations.

29. **A health article you read online or in a magazine**  
    Cue card: Describe an article about health that you read.  
    Model answer: I read an online article about the importance of sleep. It explained that sleep affects memory, mood, and physical health. The article was useful because it gave simple suggestions, such as keeping a regular bedtime and reducing screen time before sleep. I changed some habits after reading it.  
    Part 3: Is online health information reliable?  
    Answer: Some of it is reliable, but people must check the source. Advice from hospitals, doctors, or official health organizations is usually safer than random social media posts.

30. **Children behaving badly in public**  
    Cue card: Describe a time when you saw children behaving badly in public.  
    Model answer: I once saw several children running around loudly in a restaurant while their parents ignored them. It was uncomfortable because the space was crowded and waiters were carrying hot food. I think the children were just excited, but the parents should have guided them more carefully.  
    Part 3: Who should teach children public manners?  
    Answer: Parents have the main responsibility, but schools can support them. Children learn best when adults show consistent rules and good examples.

31. **A second-hand shopping website**  
    Cue card: Describe a second-hand shopping website you know.  
    Model answer: I know a second-hand platform where people sell used books, electronics, and furniture. I like it because it is cheaper and reduces waste. However, buyers need to check photos, reviews, and seller information carefully. It is useful if people shop patiently and avoid impulse buying.  
    Part 3: Why is second-hand shopping popular?  
    Answer: It saves money and supports a more sustainable lifestyle. Many people also enjoy finding unique items that are no longer sold in regular stores.

32. **An interesting story or novel**  
    Cue card: Describe a story or novel you found interesting.  
    Model answer: I read a novel about a young person leaving home and learning to become independent. I found it interesting because the character's mistakes felt realistic. The story was not only about adventure but also about responsibility. It made me think about how people grow through challenges.  
    Part 3: What makes a story interesting?  
    Answer: Strong characters, conflict, and emotional truth make a story interesting. Readers need a reason to care about what happens next.

33. **A film that made you laugh**  
    Cue card: Describe a film that made you laugh.  
    Model answer: A comedy film I watched with friends made me laugh because the humor came from everyday misunderstandings. The actors had excellent timing, and the jokes did not feel forced. Watching it with other people made it even funnier because laughter became shared.  
    Part 3: Why do people enjoy comedy?

34. **A goal you tried hard to achieve**  
    Cue card: Describe a goal you tried your best to achieve.

    Part 3: Why do some people give up their goals?

35. **A product advertisement you saw**  
    Cue card: Describe an advertisement for a product.

    Part 3: Are advertisements always honest?  
    Answer: Not always. Some exaggerate benefits or hide limitations, so consumers should compare information and read reviews before making decisions.

36. **A website you often visit**  
    Cue card: Describe a website you often visit.  
    Model answer: I often visit an educational website that offers articles, videos, and practice exercises. I use it because the content is organized and easy to search. It saves time when I need quick explanations. I also like that I can learn at my own pace.  
    Part 3: How has the internet changed learning?  
    Answer: It has made learning more flexible and accessible. However, learners also need discipline because distractions and unreliable information are common online.

### Places

37. **A place with many trees you want to visit**  
    Cue card: Describe a place with many trees that you would like to visit.  
    Model answer: I would like to visit a national forest with tall trees and quiet walking trails. I imagine the air would feel fresh and the environment peaceful. I want to go there because city life can be noisy, and a natural place would help me slow down and relax.  
    Part 3: Why are green spaces important in cities?  
    Answer: Green spaces improve air quality, reduce stress, and give people places to exercise. They also make cities more livable.

38. **An interesting building during a trip**  
    Cue card: Describe an interesting building you saw during a trip.  
    Model answer: During a trip, I saw an old railway station that had been turned into a cultural center. The building kept its historic structure but added modern galleries and cafes inside. I found it interesting because it connected the past and present in a practical way.  
    Part 3: Should old buildings be protected?  
    Answer: Yes, if they have historical or cultural value. Protection should allow careful reuse, so buildings remain useful rather than becoming empty symbols.

39. **A place where people do sports**  
    Cue card: Describe a place where you often do sports.
